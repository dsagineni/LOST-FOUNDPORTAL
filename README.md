Losing personal belongings on a college campus is a frustrating experience for students. At the same time, finding a way to return items without a reliable system can lead to confusion and misuse. Traditional methods like notice boards or word-of-mouth announcements are inefficient, lack privacy, and often fail to return items to their rightful owners. To solve this problem, we developed a Lost and Found Portal, a web-based solution designed specifically for students at our institution. The portal offers a structured digital platform with two main sections: the Lost tab and the Found tab. In the Lost tab, students who misplace an item can post details about the item type and optional descriptions. In the Found tab, students who find an item can post only the item type without giving detailed characteristics. Instead, the finder sets a minimum of five verification questions based on specific features of the item, such as brand, colour, scratches, broken parts, or distinctive markings. Claimants who think the item is theirs must answer these questions, and their responses are stored safely in the database for review later.

The portal focuses on simplicity, privacy, and security. It avoids complex features like internal chat systems, image uploads, or external moderators. This makes development easier and helps prevent misuse. Without images, ownership is confirmed through specific item details rather than visual resemblance. Also, excluding chat functionality avoids unnecessary communication. Importantly, the system does not handle the verification process automatically. Instead, the finder manually reviews the claimant’s answers and decides if they are correct. This allows flexibility in responses, making sure that differences in wording do not stop rightful owners from getting their items back. For instance, if the database says “it's blue in colour” and the claimant answers just “blue,” the finder can still accept that answer. Once the finder is happy with the claimant’s responses, the system reveals the finder’s phone number, allowing direct contact between the two. This setup makes the portal a trusted mediator, helping with item recovery while avoiding third parties and ensuring fairness and transparency.

The Lost and Found Portal offers a practical approach to solving a real issue in the college setting. By blending simplicity with functionality, it creates a reliable platform for students to recover their belongings securely and efficiently. The project highlights the importance of structured verification, confidentiality, and direct communication, making sure lost items are returned fairly. With its clear workflow—Lost tab for reporting, Found tab for discovery, securely stored claimant responses, and finder-led verification—the portal shows how

technology can tackle everyday challenges in an effective way. This initiative demonstrates the value of digital solutions in building trust and cooperation among students, while ensuring that personal belongings are safely returned to their rightful owners.
